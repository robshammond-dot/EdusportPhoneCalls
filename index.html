Travel Consultant Reference

On The Line

Phone etiquette playbook for Edusport, from kickoff to full time whistle, for every type of caller who picks up the phone to us.

🏆Every call is a first impression. Know the fixture before you play it, and never pass the buck once you have answered.

Before Kickoff

Check the profile before you say a word

The Ditronics integration means when a call comes in, the customer's Freshsales profile should pop up automatically. That pop is your team sheet, use it before you speak.

✅Profile pops up
  • Check trip history and loyalty status before greeting them
  • Note any open enquiries, quotes or bookings on file
  • Look for preferences, dietary needs or past niggles already logged
  • Reference what you see naturally, don't re-ask what's already there
⚠️Nothing pops up
  • Don't proceed on a blank screen, treat it as a missing team sheet
  • Create the customer profile in Freshsales as the call happens
  • Capture name, contact details and the reason for the call as a minimum
  • Flag repeat non-pops to the team, the integration may need a look
🗂️ For the full six-pillar approach to reading the customer behind the profile, see Beyond the Booking linked at the bottom of this page.

Match Play

Running the call itself

Four phases, same as any match: warm up, play the ball, know the score, walk off well.

🏁Kickoff
  • Answer within three rings
  • Smile, it changes your tone
  • Greet, give your name and Edusport
  • Ask how you can help
👂Active listening
  • Let them finish before replying
  • Use short verbal nods, "I see"
  • Note key details as they speak
  • Ask before you assume
📝The recap
  • Summarise what was agreed
  • Confirm the next step and owner
  • Repeat dates, names and numbers
  • Check they have nothing to add
🏁Full time
  • Thank them for their time
  • Confirm they are happy to end
  • Let the caller hang up first
  • Log the call notes right after
💡 Golden rule: if you cannot repeat the call back in one sentence, you have not really listened.

Know The Fixture

Different types of calls, different game plans

Not every call is the same match. Read what you're playing before you set your approach.

🎟️ New booking enquiry
First contact, exploring an event or trip
Focus: qualify the trip, understand the occasion, don't rush to quote before you understand what matters to them
🔁 Existing booking / follow-up query
Something already in motion needs an update
Focus: pull up the booking first, know the status before you say "let me check"
💳 Payment or invoicing call
Deposits, balances, refunds, invoice queries
Focus: get the figures right the first time, confirm amounts and dates back to the customer before ending the call
🚩 Complaint or issue
Something has gone wrong, or feels like it has
Focus: acknowledge before you explain, resolve or escalate, never leave the customer holding the problem
🌍 General info or travel advice
Visas, fixtures, packages, no immediate booking
Focus: still worth a great call, today's advice call is often next season's booking
🏅 Loyalty / repeat traveller check-in
Proactive calls to a regular or referring customer
Focus: bring the calendar to them, don't wait for them to ask what's coming up

Team Selection

Reading who's on the other end of the line

Same playbook, different opponent. Adjust your approach to who you're talking to, not just what they're asking.

🌱
The new client
First cap, no history on file yet

No trip history to lean on, so the call itself has to build the trust a returning customer already has. Slow down, explain things a repeat traveller wouldn't need explained, and start the profile properly from this first touch.

"Since this is your first trip with us, let me quickly explain how the package works and what's included."
🏅
The repeat client
Knows the club, expects to be known

They've earned the right to not repeat themselves. Reference their history before they have to bring it up, and treat re-asking logged preferences as a miss, not a formality.

"I see you stayed at the Royal Palm last time, keen to do the same again or try something different this time round?"
⏱️
The follow-up call
Extra time on something already underway

They're calling because something is still open. Lead with the status, not with "can I take your details again". If it's been sitting too long, own that honestly rather than making them chase it a second time.

"I can see your quote is still with our supplier for confirmation, let me chase that now while you're on the line."
🟥
The angry client
Red card moment, temperature is high

Let them get it out before you respond. Don't defend, don't explain process first, acknowledge the frustration is valid before doing anything else. Then move to resolving it.

"I hear you, this isn't the experience we want you to have, let me look into this properly and sort it out."

No Substitutions

You are Edusport on this call, own it

Whoever answers the phone is Edusport in that moment, not just the person who happens to hold that customer's file.

🚫 Never say: "that's not my client", "that's not my issue", or "you'll need to call back and ask for so-and-so". The customer doesn't experience Edusport as departments, they experience it as one team.
🤝Instead, do this
  • Take ownership of the call, even if the file sits with someone else
  • Say "let me find that out for you" rather than passing the caller on
  • Escalate internally, quietly, without making it the customer's problem
  • If a handover is genuinely needed, brief the colleague before transferring, don't make the customer repeat themselves
📣Sounds like this
  • "That's usually handled by a colleague, let me get them looped in and I'll keep you updated."
  • "I don't have that detail in front of me, give me a moment to pull it up rather than transferring you cold."
  • "I'll take ownership of getting this sorted, here's what happens next."

Red Flags

Qualify these before the call moves on

⚠No customer profile popping up and no attempt made to create one
⚠A returning customer being asked questions already answered on a previous call
⚠"Not my client" or "not my issue" language, in any form, to a customer
⚠A call ending without a clear recap of what was agreed and who owns the next step
⚠An angry client met with process explanation before acknowledgement
⚠A transfer made without briefing the receiving colleague first

Why This Matters

🏆
First impressions decide the season
A new client's first call sets whether they book once or become a regular.
🔁
Repeat travellers remember everything
Sporting calendars repeat every year, so does the experience you gave last time.
🛡️
Ownership prevents complaints
Most complaints escalate because a customer felt passed around, not because of the original issue.
📋
The recap is the record
What gets recapped on the call is what should get logged straight after it.
